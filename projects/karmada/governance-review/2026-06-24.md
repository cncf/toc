### Governance Summary

Karmada provides a multi-cluster control plane which manages placing Kubernetes resources on a selection of member clusters. The control plane itself is built on modified Kubernetes api-server and controller-manager components and it intercepts local placement of resources and places them on remote clusters instead. The project also provides a means to query for resources from across the fleet and a unified authentication/authorization system for modifying resources across the fleet.

Governance and other community concerns are documented in the [karmada-io/community repo](https://github.com/karmada-io/community) and the project itself is contained in one repo at [karmada-io/karmada](https://github.com/karmada-io/karmada). 13 maintainers from 7 different companies are listed in Karmada's [MAINTAINERS doc](https://github.com/karmada-io/community/blob/main/MAINTAINERS.md).

The project is led by its maintainers in a [Maintainer Council style](https://contribute.cncf.io/projects/best-practices/governance/templates/governance-maintainer).

### Decisions and Role Assignments

**Document how the project makes decisions on leadership roles, contribution
acceptance, requests to the CNCF, and changes to governance or project goals.**
**Incubating:** Suggested | **Graduated:** Required

Most roles are assigned to contributors through a nomination and majority vote of current holders of that role, as described in [community-membership.md](https://github.com/karmada-io/community/blob/main/community-membership.md). But becoming a maintainer requires a 2/3 vote of current maintainers.

Contribution acceptance is dependent on review and approval from existing community members as documented in [CONTRIBUTING](https://github.com/karmada-io/community/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md)

Changes to governance and project goals require a 2/3 vote of maintainers as well, as documented in [GOVERNANCE](https://github.com/karmada-io/community/blob/main/GOVERNANCE.md#voting).

All maintainers may open support requests with CNCF as documented in [GOVERNANCE](https://github.com/karmada-io/community/blob/main/GOVERNANCE.md#cncf-resources).

**Document how role, function-based members, or sub-teams are assigned,
onboarded, and removed for specific teams (example: Security Response
Committee).**
**Incubating:** Suggested | **Graduated:** Required

- Special Interest Groups are for deeper investigation of scoped domains and are formed by a 2/3 vote of maintainers. They are encouraged to have multiple leads. Documentation [here](https://github.com/karmada-io/community/blob/main/sigs/README.md).
- A security response team is chartered [here](https://github.com/karmada-io/community/blob/main/sigs/README.md) and fully defined [here](https://github.com/karmada-io/community/blob/main/security-team/README.md).
